Jessica Ip

Hi there! I’m a Canadian student researcher from UBC with interests in perceptual processes, attention, information systems, and visualization.

I recently completed my bachelor's degree in Cognitive Systems at the University of British Columbia. For the past four years, I’ve had the honour of collaborating with an eclectic group of talented researchers and developers at the UBC Visual Cognition Lab, where I investigated the human visual perception of correlation in various visualization designs. During this time I was a UX researcher in a cross-institutional collaboration between the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems and the UBC Sensory Perception and Research Group on the Haptipedia project. My earlier research experiences were also in information visualization and user research on the ValueCharts project in the Laboratory for Computational Intelligence. Interestingly, a central theme of my research experiences has been in vision. My earliest research internship experience from high school has been at the UBC Department of Ophthalmology & Visual Sciences in the VGH Eye Care Center, where I learned wet lab techniques and studied retinitis pigmentosa and macular degeneration in Xenopus laevis photoreceptors.

Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of human-computer interaction, information visualization, and perceptual processing. Beyond my general fascination with the intricacies of the brain, I’m especially curious about the perceptual mechanisms that guide decision-making and how these processes could inform the design of new information and visualization systems.
